SUBJECT: Complete Identification of Tomb Guardian

Following the recovery of the first cartouche fragment from Chamber
KH-III (see Scroll I translation), our epigraphy team completed
analysis of the secondary cartouche recovered from a collapsed
storage niche in the eastern gallery.

The rank designation had been deliberately obscured in all primary
sources accessible within the main tomb complex. However, the
ancillary cataloguing record discovered beneath floor tile E-14
preserved the complete ordinal marker in hieratic script.

Professor Henriksen's translation confirms that the guardian's
ordinal designation translates directly as 7th - indicating
he held the seventh position within the relevant office of the
Temple of Thoth. This numerical designation carried special
significance, as the number seven was considered sacred in the
theological framework of the Heliopolitan priesthood.

When combined with the personal identifier recovered from Scroll I
and the office terminology preserved elsewhere in the archive,
the guardian's complete ceremonial title can be reconstructed
in full. The bridge-mark notation documented across multiple
sites of the same period should be preserved between each part.
