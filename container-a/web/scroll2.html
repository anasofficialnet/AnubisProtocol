Scroll of the Second Inscription

RECOVERED FROM THE EASTERN GALLERY

𓁟 The Hierarchy of Temple Offices

The Temple of Thoth maintained a strict hierarchy among its servants. Each was assigned a numerical rank based on their order of initiation, followed by their sacred role. The ranking system used ordinal numbers to distinguish between servants of equal standing, ensuring no confusion in the chain of authority.

This scroll, recovered from the eastern gallery alongside votive offerings to Ma'at, describes the organizational structure in detail. The ranks began with the 1st Overseer and descended through the various levels of archivists, translators, and chroniclers who maintained the temple's vast library.

One title appears again and again in the surviving records: scribe. The guardian of this tomb belonged to that office, though his precise number has been scratched away by careful hands.

Excavation field notes suggest that additional documentation exists in the tomb's ancillary records - perhaps in surveyor logs or cataloguing notes that were not meant for the main archive.

𓂝 The Recovered Papyrus

During the Season XII campaign, a papyrus fragment was recovered from beneath a collapsed shelf in the eastern gallery. The fragment appears to contain a single inscription in a style consistent with ceremonial records of the same period. Forensic analysis suggests the inscription was deliberately concealed within the fibers of the papyrus itself.

The fragment has been photographed and preserved below. Dr. Henriksen's team notes that the ancients were known to hide messages within the very material of their writings - a technique the modern world calls steganography. The key to reading such hidden texts was always guarded by the patron deity of scribes and writing.

Papyrus Fragment EG-7 — "What is hidden in the fiber, the patron of scribes reveals."

𓂝 The Lost Ranks

Many of the rank designations recovered from other tombs follow a consistent pattern: an ordinal number indicating position, followed by the word for the office itself. Common examples from the era include titles such as "3rd_keeper", "12th_chronicler", and similar formations.

The guardian of this tomb held a particularly significant position. References found in the Treasury of Karnak describe him as one whose rank placed him at a sacred number - a number revered across multiple ancient civilizations for its mystical properties. Unfortunately, the precise rank title referenced here has been deliberately removed from all primary sources.

"What the scrolls withhold, the earth remembers." - Field Note, Excavation Season XII
