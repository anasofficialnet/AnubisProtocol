𓁟 The Legend of the Seventh Scribe

In the age before the first dynasty, when the gods still walked among mortals, there existed a protocol - ancient beyond measure, powerful beyond comprehension. The great Thoth, architect of all knowledge, inscribed it upon walls of living stone deep beneath the sands of Saqqara.

Khasem, the Seventh Scribe, was chosen by Anubis himself to guard the protocol. For forty years he transcribed the sacred texts onto papyrus woven from midnight reeds, encoding each passage with layered rites that only the patient could unravel. His dedication was absolute - for the protocol contained the weight of eternity, the measure of the soul against the feather of Ma'at.

The protocol speaks of three fragments. One hides in the whispers between servers, another sleeps in the dust between inscriptions, and the last waits in the structured depths of the archive. Only when they are spoken in procession does the balance listen.

Those who seek the artifact must prove patience above all else. The scales of Ma'at do not yield to haste. Do not trust the indexed paths - many chambers were built to mislead the unworthy.

𓄃 The Prophecy of the Three Gates

The priests of the Seventh Gate foresaw that one day a seeker would arrive - one who could read the language of machines and speak the tongue of the ancients. They left this prophecy carved into the inner sanctum wall:

"When the three fragments speak as one,
and the seeker returns unchanged before the scales,
the path to the weighing chamber shall open.
But first - learn his name, for you cannot enter
the house of a stranger."

The prophecy suggests that knowing the scribe's identity is essential before the final passage can be attempted. His name and his office were deliberately fractured across multiple records to prevent unworthy hands from assembling them by chance alone.
